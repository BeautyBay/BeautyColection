Keto diet: pros and cons

We decided to tell you in simple words about all the pros and cons of keto diet . Have you ever heard of it? In short, it is a diet during which you need to almost completely abandon carbohydrates, the main diet consists of protein and fat. Let's go into more detail about what it happens to the body during such a diet.

A little background: keto diet was developed in the early 20th century for the treatment of children with epilepsy. Giving up carbohydrates triggered processes that inhibited the development of seizures, and allowed you to reduce the amount of drugs, and sometimes completely abandon them. It was noticed that during treatment, patients began to lose weight. For weight loss, keto diet actively began to be used only in the late 1990s.

The keto diet triggers the ketosis process. What is ketosis? Normally, the body burns glucose to get energy. When do we stop consuming carbohydrates â€” the main source of glucose, the body begins to consume stored fats, converting them into fatty acids and ketone bodies (acetone, acetoacetic acid and beta-oximabutyric acid). Ketone bodies enter the brain and become a substitute for glucose, i.e. a new source of energy. That's the whole point cattily.

It has become especially popular due to the fact that it does not require fasting. You will have to give up almost everything that contains carbohydrates, but fats and proteins can be consumed in normal amounts.Â 

During the keto diet, the fat content is 75% of the diet, protein-20%, carbohydrates-only 5%.

  • you can quickly lose weight on a keto diet;
  • no need to adhere to a separate food;
  • you can eat at any time of the day, even after 18: 00 (within reasonable limits, because no one has canceled the daily calorie allowance).

Minuses:

  • you will have to give up most fruits and grains, the body will be deficient in vitamins and minerals. celluloses;
  • nutrition is unbalanced, especially affected by the lack of useful "slow" carbohydrates. This diet is not suitable for long-term use. (you can quickly throw off 2-3 kg, but it is not worth sitting on it further);
  • at first, you will suffer from headaches, nausea, and dizzinesses;
  • it can cause constipation, vomiting, and kidney stones;
  • disrupts the work of the gastrointestinal tract;
  • the smell of acetone from the mouth, as well as from urine and from the body (due to the increased content of ketone bodies in the body).
